MIKE GERSTENFELD, M.D.

MEDICAL DIRECTOR

Mike Gerstenfeld, MD is Medical Director and Founder of Integrated Medical Centers. He received his Bachelor's Degree in Chemistry from Kenyon College, and his Medical Degree from F.E.U. School of Medicine. He completed his surgical internship at Maimonides Medical Center, Brooklyn, New York and continued his training in Internal Medicine at Danbury Hospital/Yale University School of Medicine Program. As an exchange fellow, he studied Traditional and Eastern Medicine at The Second Military Medical University in Shanghai, China.

Dr. Gerstenfeld integrates traditional and complementary medicine, including the latest advancements in digital health. His clinical interests include physical medicine and managing patients with Type 2 and Type 2>Type 1 diabetes. He also cares for the men and women injured by the events of 9/11.

His Designations include:

  • Civil Surgeon, United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), Department of Homeland Security.
  • 9/11 World Trade Center National Provider.
  • U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) National Provider.

His awards include:

  • Health Hero Award for Innovation by the Governor of Connecticut.
  • Patients' Choice Award 2012 for outstanding patient care and expertise.
  • Top 10 Doctor Award 2014
  • Top 50 in Digital Health 2017
